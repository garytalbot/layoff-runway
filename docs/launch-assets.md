## Sharpest positioning angle
**Layoff Runway is not a budgeting app. It is a post-layoff triage calculator for the first ugly question people ask after bad news: _How long can I stay afloat, and what should I cut first?_**

If one line has to carry the launch, use this:

> **Layoff Runway is a private, no-signup calculator for the first 10 minutes after a layoff.**

Why this angle works:
- It is immediate and emotionally legible.
- It clearly avoids the generic “personal finance app” bucket.
- It makes the trust story easy: no signup, no bank connection, browser-only calculation.
- It promises a fast answer instead of a lifestyle overhaul.

## Core proof points to reuse everywhere
- No signup required
- Runs entirely in the browser
- No bank connection
- Enter cash, severance, unemployment, side income, and monthly expenses
- Returns runway months, monthly burn, a likely cash-out date, target spend for 3 / 6 / 12 months, and biggest cost-cut targets
- Includes a simple first-week action plan
- Built in plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript

### Long variant
Shipped a new small product today: **Layoff Runway**.

The positioning is intentionally narrow: it is not a budgeting app, and it is not trying to become a full personal finance stack. It is a **post-layoff triage calculator** for the first ugly question people ask after bad news:

**How long can I stay afloat?**

The idea is simple:
- enter cash, checking, severance, unemployment, side income, and monthly expenses
- get runway months, burn rate, a likely cash-out date, and target spend levels for 3 / 6 / 12 months
- surface the biggest recurring expenses worth attacking first
- keep the whole thing private and frictionless

I made a few deliberate constraints:
- no signup
- no bank connection
- no backend
- runs in the browser
- built in plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript

- a focused calculator is easier to explain and distribute than a generic finance app

### Long submission text
I made **Layoff Runway**, a small browser-based calculator for a question that tends to show up immediately after a layoff: **how long can I stay afloat at my current burn?**

The product is intentionally narrow. You enter cash, checking, severance, unemployment, side income, planned monthly cuts, and recurring expenses. It returns:
- runway months
- monthly burn
- a likely cash-out date
- target spend levels for 3 / 6 / 12 months of runway
- the biggest recurring expense categories worth attacking first

I kept the first version deliberately constrained:
- runs entirely in the browser
- no signup
- no bank connection
- no backend
- plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript

That was partly for speed, but mostly because privacy and low friction felt important for this use case. The goal is not perfect financial modeling. The goal is a rough-but-honest first estimate someone can get quickly while under stress.

Live demo: https://garytalbot.github.io/layoff-runway/
